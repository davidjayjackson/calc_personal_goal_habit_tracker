# Personal Goal & Habit Tracker

A goal-setting and daily habit tracking template to define what matters, build consistent routines, review your progress weekly, and visualise an entire year of habit data at a glance.

## 📋 Overview

Goals without systems rarely stick. This template pairs meaningful goal-setting with the daily habit tracking that actually drives progress. Log your habits every day, run weekly reviews to reflect and recalibrate, celebrate milestones, and see the full picture of your year in a heatmap-style annual view. It's designed to keep you honest, motivated, and moving forward.

## 📄 Sheets

| Sheet | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| `Goals Overview` | Up to 10 goals with category, target date, priority, and progress |
| `Daily Habits` | Monthly habit grid — rows are habits, columns are days |
| `Weekly Review` | Structured reflection prompts for each week |
| `Milestones` | Log of key achievements with dates and notes |
| `Year at a Glance` | Heatmap-style view showing habit consistency across all 52 weeks |

## 🚀 Getting Started

1. Open `personal-goal-habit-tracker.ots` in LibreOffice Calc.
2. Define your goals in **Goals Overview** — be specific about what success looks like.
3. Add your daily habits (3–7 is a manageable start) to the **Daily Habits** sheet.
4. Each morning or evening, mark habits as complete in **Daily Habits**.
5. Every Sunday, spend 10 minutes on the **Weekly Review**.
6. Record significant achievements in **Milestones** as they happen.
7. Check **Year at a Glance** monthly to see patterns forming.

## 📝 How to Use Each Sheet

### Goals Overview
Define up to 10 goals with the following columns:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Goal | Clear, specific description of what you want to achieve |
| Category | Health, Finance, Learning, Relationships, Career, Personal, Other |
| Priority | High / Medium / Low |
| Target Date | When you want to achieve this by |
| Success Metric | How you'll know you've achieved it (measurable) |
| % Complete | Update this manually as you make progress |
| Status | On Track / At Risk / Achieved / Paused |
| Notes | Current next action or blockers |

Goals are colour-coded by status: green for On Track, amber for At Risk, blue for Achieved, grey for Paused.

### Daily Habits
The core daily tracking sheet:

- **Rows** = individual habits (e.g., Exercise, Read 20 mins, No alcohol, Meditate)
- **Columns** = days of the current month (1–31)
- Mark a cell with `1` or `✓` when you complete a habit for the day
- Leave it blank (or enter `0`) for days you miss
- **Green** cells = completed; **Red** cells = missed; blank = not applicable (future dates)
- A completion percentage is calculated per habit at the end of each row
- A daily completion score is shown at the bottom of each column

### Weekly Review
A structured reflection sheet with weekly prompts:

- **What went well this week?**
- **What was challenging?**
- **Which habits did I maintain?**
- **Which habits did I miss, and why?**
- **What's my focus for next week?**
- **Progress check on goals** — rate each goal 1–5 on momentum

There's one section per week of the year. Fill it in every Sunday in about 10–15 minutes.

### Milestones
Log significant achievements as they happen:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Date | When the milestone was reached |
| Milestone | Description of the achievement |
| Related Goal | Which goal this milestone supports |
| How I Felt | A brief note on your reaction or reflection |

Over time this becomes a motivating record of how far you've come.

### Year at a Glance
- A full-year heatmap with 52 columns (weeks) and rows for each habit.
- Cell colour intensity reflects how consistent you were that week (0–7 days).
- Darker green = more consistent; white/light = low consistency.
- Provides a powerful bird's-eye view of your habits across the whole year.
- Updates automatically from the Daily Habits data as the year progresses.

## 💡 Tips

- **Start with 3–5 habits maximum.** Adding too many at once leads to overwhelm. Build momentum first, then add more.
- **Mark habits at the same time each day** — either morning (for planning) or evening (for reviewing). Consistency in tracking builds the habit of tracking itself.
- **Be honest in your weekly review.** It's a private document — the insights only help if they're accurate.
- **Green/red contrast is intentional** — streaks of green are satisfying; streaks of red are a clear signal to reflect and adjust.
- Revisit your Goals Overview at the start of each month — priorities shift, and that's fine. Update statuses and target dates as needed.
